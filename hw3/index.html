Part 2
  • Customer Segments: Separated into two parts. First is the primary market for young boys who like the toys, and second is the niche market for all genders interested in the role playing game
  • Value Propositions: The company provides toys with the allure of the star wars brand, at lower costs than the branded alternative. On top of that, the toys can be used in a variety of ways, including being used as figurines for a role playing game
  • Customer Relationships: The website offers customers their choice in toys, and processes these transactions safely and efficiently. In order to build trust with the customer, it is important that the toys are of decent quality, as well as reasonably valued.
  • Revenue Streams: Revenue is primarily made through the selling of merchandise through the website, but secondary revenue can also be made by allowing advertisers to purchase advertising space on the website.
  • Key Resources: Key resources would be IT people to manage the site; Customer service representatives to easily file claims and suggestions and enough raw resources to manufacture the toys.
  • Key Activities: Provide a well functioning and safe website; Draw in customers through advertising campaign; Provide good customer service so that customers return to the company again.
  • Key Partnerships: Partnership with a manufacturing company would significantly lower manufacturing costs; A partnership with the role playing game, in order to further associate the game with the toys; A partnership with a payment processor such as paypal would allow a safe way to process payments, perhaps at a reduced cost; finally a partnership with a shipping company would allow better rates on shipping, perhaps priority status on shipments.
  • Cost Structure: Would have to pay for marketing; Manufacture of the toys; perhaps hire somebody to maintain the website; technical costs associated with running a website, such as hosting and domain name.
